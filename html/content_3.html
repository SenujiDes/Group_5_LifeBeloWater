CONSERVE AND SUSTAINABLY USE THE OCEANS, SEAS AND MARINE RESOURCES FOR SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT.

Things to Do Targets



Things to do


When purchasing seafood, look for labels such as the Marine Stewardship Council (MSC) or Aquaculture Stewardship Council (ASC) certification, which indicate that the seafood has been sustainably sourced.

Purchase seafood from local aquaculture operations that follow sustainable practices and prioritize environmental stewardship.

Minimize food waste by buying only what you need and properly storing leftovers. Compost food scraps instead of throwing them away to reduce landfill waste and greenhouse gas emissions.

Practice water conservation in your daily life to reduce the strain on freshwater resources. This indirectly supports aquaculture sustainability by ensuring adequate water supplies for aquaculture operations.

Support policies and regulations that promote sustainable aquaculture practices, such as habitat protection, pollution control, and responsible fish farming standards.

Learn about the environmental impacts of aquaculture and share this knowledge with others. Encourage friends, family, and community members to make informed choices that support sustainable seafood.

Get involved in local or global conservation efforts focused on protecting aquatic ecosystems and promoting sustainable aquaculture practices.

Targets


Reduce personal seafood consumption:

Education and outreach

Conservation volunteering

Promote eco-friendly aquaculture


Aim to switch X% (e.g., 30%) of your aquaculture product purchases to eco-friendly options, such as RAS or IMTA, within the next year.

Support research and innovation


Allocate X amount (e.g., $500) towards supporting research and innovation in sustainable aquaculture technologies or practices within the next year.

Engagement with retailers and restaurants


Commit to engaging with X (e.g., 5) seafood retailers or restaurants within the next six months to advocate for sustainable seafood options and transparent sourcing practices.

IMPLEMENT AND ENFORCE INTERNATIONAL SEA LAW


Enhance the conservation and sustainable use of oceans and their resources by implementing international law as reflected in the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea, which provides the legal framework for the conservation and sustainable use of oceans and their resources, as recalled in paragraph 158 of “The future we want”.
